The Curse of Mankind…

When a child becomes too stubborn or steps out of bounds, it is a mother’s right to scald or punish this child. As she brought this child into the world, so she should be able to take them out of it. This ideology has been imbedded within countless eras and as advancements grew, so did the people. Yet though these people no longer took physical shapes or physical presences, they still remained, still waged wars and were still unable to ease their boredom. This was a never ending cycle until one being, a being who had amassed enough power to destroy all of her brethren, made a decision. This being decided that for their greater good, it was her right to destroy her friends and family in order for peace to be brought down upon the world. Through these means, she ruthlessly tore down her fellow comrades, destroying their very being and eradicating them from existence. Once all beings were dealt with and no traces remained, this war goddess rested. Licked her wounds and began to plan her new existence. She no longer had friends to talk to, someone to confide in, or anyone to bring her joy. She embodied the true meaning of being alone. For it was only her mind that kept her company for all these eons, yet soon she recovered.

Upon her recovery she examined the world, it was devastated. The war she had recklessly waged had unfortunately destroyed much of once was her home. However, given that she had cast off the shackles of her previous humanity, she sought out to create a new civilization. This civilization would be perfect, she thought. It would be free from the treacherous power struggles and ruthless killing of her previous kin, as she was able to create a “perfect” society. This great being mustered up all of her energy, forcing herself to her limits and she reshaped the world into a beautiful utopia and laid the seeds for a new and enlightened mankind to be formed. However through this great feat, she had become again recklessly brought herself to her limits. So she slept, knowing that when she awoke, a new society will have formed. It would be thriving and it would be perfect.

When she awakened, she leaped out into the air looking down upon her wonderful creations in an attempt to take in all of their progression at once and to assess their current state. However, these creatures were not the beautiful and divine manifestation she had sought to create. No, these creatures were even worse than her brethren once were. They were unrefined grovel that had no place in this world and she was sickened that all of her hard work had been put into such creatures. She had to work at once, had to rectify her mistake and start anew with a better and more beautiful slate of creatures. So again, this powerful war goddess prepared to massacre an entire population. These weak and unknowing citizens were soon to fall, for these creatures were filth and deserved no less than a salvage assault from this mother’s rage. She sent down a manifestation of chaos to do her bidding to ensure that no beings were left for her next project.

Within an instant of this manifestation’s release, harsh rain struck down from above a great western continent. People began to scatter, fleeing from the sting of cold water, almost like acid. The streets began to fill and debris that once lay dormant was now carried in a torrent of waves, almost trembling in the wake of the water’s power. The little continent was being gutted alive as trees were uprooted from the ground and buildings were broken, being put on their knees as they let out a genocidal scream of creaks, almost begging to be saved from this monstrous entity. This western continent was decimated; however it was not the only thing to fall that day. Multiple major cities and rural environments were destroyed in the torrents and very few lives were spared. But this was not the end of their torment. No, there would be much more.

Off in the distant north, a continent began to quake, the fiber’s of its being was shaken down to the land’s core. Cracks slowly began to form, splitting buildings in two and causing smoke to rise. Blood curdling screams could be heard trailing off into the distance, sharing their last contribution to the world in the form of terror. Soon cracks began to overtake the continent, causing all of the structures to either fall or be lost into the depths of nothingness. Soon all that was heard was that of a slow but constant crackling. Red hot lava had been regurgitate from the land, spewing back the lost belongings and bodies that had just been swallowed, ensuring that their very essence suffered. All that remained of this continent and its suffering was an eerie cloud of ash that covered the entirety of the content, drifting up in plumes. However from a distance one could see the faint outlines of a skull that appeared to have a crack splitting down the center.

Lastly, the east was ravaged; however the populace was the most interesting. These people were religious, zealots and worshiped some unknown figure. Though this figure was not the supreme goddess, no it took the form of a patriarchal deity which showed it’d power through force. Such beings worshiping a fake and faulty god should be crushed. So torrents of waters were sent to play with these zealots, giving them the worst death of all. The east’s people did not flee, they did not cower, they were not afraid… at first. At first the waters began to swell, causing it to swirl and crash into their chapels, as if clawing at them and toying with its prey. Their faith held strong, for those brief moments. But then the contents of the water began to take on a soft red color that began to slowly become darker. “Repent!” yelled a priest unknowingly gazing into his peons; however his voice only fell on deaf ears. Everyone in this illustrious gilded chapel had realized what the ocean water had mixed with, blood. Its manifestation was both cruel and maniacal and as its pitch finally hit a solid blood red color, it began to seep through the walls. Slowly creeping in and covering the floors. The religious zealots that were once calm began to panic as their soft leather shoes were now drenched in a fowl, tainted blood. The blood of those that had fallen before them; the blood that would destroy them. The blood took hours to fill the chamber, however no matter how hard the peons tried to break down the glass or the doors to try to escape; only more blood flowed in. There was no escape from their pursuer. Not even their religious deity could save them now.

One thing remained, but this entity was eternal. For it was the being that once gave life to such beautiful beings, but these beings had been tainted. Life forms now devoid of the beauty, devoid of purpose and devoid of a soul. This mother now sought to take back what had once been given, to return her children to her fiery depths in hopes that she could start anew. Maybe the next assortment of creatures will listen, for when her screams fill the air yet fall on deaf ears; rage begins to bellow from within. The fury unleashed cannot be stopped. However through the ashes of destruction life can be born anew. This newly formed life can be forged pure, purer than any refined metal so that their essence, their true soul, shall remain untainted. Or so this being hoped, yet creation through malice shall never create peace. Her want to start over by destroying her once beautiful creation was now tainted by her former destruction. So to ensure that her influence be purged from existence, she created her last creation, a world without complexity, a world without a purpose for as she forged this new world, she took her own life, assuming that her tainted wishes were the reason for all of mankind’s previous mistakes.

The next society was different in a way. They formed as different races and populated different areas. They developed and expanded, soon finding each other and through war and trade, these countries grew. Interactions were rough; certain populations of people were treated poorly by those with greater resources or superior technology. However, a true insight was made. For mankind’s true curse is that mankind is inherently evil.

 

The time traveler, and the old wise man.

There once was a man named Cleion, and on his many journeys he happened upon a man. The man had no name, as it was lost in the ages of past, but he had many secrets to tell, though at a price. Cleion, still young and youthful, had no care for his body and his superiority complex brought him to think he was invincible. But as he continued to find through his many long journeys and awkward endeavors, he was not as strong, smart, or talented as he was first brought to believe. His fights with Hercules left him scarred and battered, with Hercules still in his prime. He even attempted to go for the best of the underworld and fight Hatis, but even then, with one of the weaker gods, he was bested and again brought to the brink of his life. But he has this oddness about him, his sheer will to believe that he would never die, that kept him alive. No one knows why he was spared, and many feel he should be dead. But on he lives, until the meeting of this old man....

Cleion walks up to a warried traveler, appearing to be in his late 90s and unable to move, sitting on a stone slab and peering out into the vast canyons that run through the land. Orange sand glistened as it seamlessly was lifted into the air and wove around into the sky. Cleion asks the old man his name, with his poorly obtained manners. "Dear sir, give me your name or face the invincible cleion, master of this realm and ruler of all lowly creatures. The old man looks at him, and a twinkle in his eyes sparks something in Cleion. :I am the wind, the sand, the ages. I have no true being, but am eternal. Do not question the name of my being, as the depths of time have all but erased it." Cleion looked at the old man puzzled, then said with an inquisitive voice. "But then what may i call you, and what can you offer me as service?" The old man chuckles, looking Cleion dead in the eye. "I offer you nothing but knowledge and wisdom far beyond that with which any human knows. I am the three evils, and I will chose the third, to tell you of all the evil the others have stolen from their perception.
